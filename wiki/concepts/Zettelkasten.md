# Zettelkasten

"Slip-box" note-taking method developed by German sociologist [[Niklas Luhmann]], who used ~90,000 index cards to produce 70+ books and 400+ papers.

## Core Principles

- Separate raw sources from processed knowledge
- Rewrite ideas in **your own words** (not copy-paste)
- Create links between cards — connections between ideas are more valuable than the ideas themselves
- Designed for compound growth over a lifetime

## Key Difference from LLM Wiki

Surface similarities: both separate raw from processed knowledge, both prioritize connections, both compound. But the fundamental difference is **who does the writing**:

- **Zettelkasten**: You write → the friction of articulation *is* the thinking
- **LLM Wiki**: LLM writes → friction eliminated → you read the output

Extended Brain argues this distinction is critical via the concept of [[Hormesis]]: eliminating cognitive friction may produce beautiful maps but weakens the traveler.
