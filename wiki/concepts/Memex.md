# Memex

Hypothetical device described by [[Vannevar Bush]] in his 1945 article "As We May Think" in The Atlantic Monthly. A desk-sized machine where an individual could store all books, records, and communications, search them rapidly, and create "associative trails" — linked sequences of documents with personal annotations.

## Core Insight

The human mind operates by **association**, not alphabetical indexing. Bush's core idea: connections between documents are as valuable as the documents themselves.

## Legacy

- Inspired Douglas Engelbart → mouse, personal computing
- Inspired Ted Nelson → "hypertext" (1965)
- Inspired Tim Berners-Lee → World Wide Web (1989)

## Connection to LLM Wiki

Karpathy observes the web became public and chaotic, not the private curated space Bush envisioned. LLM Wiki is closer to Bush's original vision: private, actively curated, connections as valuable as content.

**Bush's unsolved puzzle**: Who does the maintenance? Creating associative trails and keeping everything consistent was tedious manual labor. LLMs solve this — they don't get bored, don't forget to update cross-references, and can touch 15+ files in one operation.
